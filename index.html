Project Echo

Hearing aids have long struggled to effectively filter unwanted environmental noise while preserving the clarity of speech. For millions of users, busy environments like restaurants, public transport, and workplaces render their devices frustrating rather than helpful. Traditional noise suppression methods often degrade speech quality in the process, forcing users to choose between hearing everything or missing important parts.

As AI Team Lead on Project Echo, I am directing the research and development of a machine learning pipeline designed to tackle this problem. This involved evaluating existing noise reduction approaches, identifying their shortcomings, and designing an architecture that could separate speech from complex, overlapping noise sources in real time. I coordinated a team of developers, setting technical direction while managing timelines and project finances as finance lead.

I presented this project to IBM representatives at an industrial showcase, receiving strong interest from industry professionals. Beyond the technical outcomes, leading Project Echo sharpened my ability to communicate complex technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders and presenting skills.
